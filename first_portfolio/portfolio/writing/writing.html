Foreword: An Apology to M

Apr. 27, 2016

M,

 You left. The reasons why were obvious: me. Specifically my ignorance and incapacity to get involved in our relationship. Things just weren't going anywhere, yet you tried. You tried to salvage what was so beautiful about the first times we got to know each other. What could've been years of incredible memories turned to weeks of dry and dead interactions.

 Self-loathing and self-pity were things I carried with me long before I met you. I don't know why I wasn't able to change my perspective on life once we had met. Fear most likely. Everything was so new. What I had found in you was so different compared to everyone and everything else. I knew very well that we were something I had to hold onto for as long as I could.

 Plans for a brighter future were canceled and are now long gone. Yet I, like a stubborn child, still hold on. When will it end? When do I let go? I should’ve long ago but something within me is afraid of erasing everything and starting over. There are just so many things I look back on and realize I had no clue as to what I was doing. I can only hope you've let go and moved on. God forbid my negligence still haunts you. I wouldn't be able to forgive myself if I were to find out you still want more, despite all my mistakes. I wish this were all a dream. You can wake up and move on from those.

Yours,

D

Shy

Outcast

Without a place of belonging. An outcast in the universe. A nuisance to God himself.

I realized I hadn't been true to myself. I had been telling God to keep me here for some reason but I did not know what for. Why did I insist?

"Stop!" I said.

I vanished.

Here I am and there I was. Existing without need and wanting more than needed. I had no ambition or sense of direction. My self-identity was a shattered one pulling in different directions. Who am I anyways? A joke amongst my peers. A joke to my family. A joke to the world. I was nothing more than dirt. I had willed it all this way.

Dependent: Individual shows an extreme need to be taken care of that leads to fears of separation, and passive and clinging behavior. Core issue is the need to be parented by others (i.e. avoid growing up/becoming self-sufficient).

Borderline: Individual shows a generalized pattern of instability in interpersonal relationship, self-image, and observable emotions, and significant impulsiveness. Core issue is an inability to regulate emotions.

For this I am sorry and may never say it directly towards you, but through written word I can say it all.

I fall the to the floor face first and crawl to the door as I seek to escape. It's as if your name has been branded straight into my skull and brain because every time I look in the mirror I remember the face I shared with the world when I was much happier for being with you.

How I want to scrape away and grate every last fiber bearing your mark but to do so would destroy a part of me in the process. For this reason I control my impulse and cause myself to suffer indefinitely. To suffer because it feels good. To suffer because I like it.
