Performance Title: Pica


Part of the "Where All is Gold, Steel, and Gems, and Light" group exhibition at Otis College of Art and Design


Date: circa November 2013

Pica (ˈpīkə) noun: a tendency or craving to eat substances other than normal food (such as clay, plaster, or ashes), occurring during childhood or pregnancy, or as a symptom of disease.

Purpose: Read to the audience things that cause them to contemplate existence while the performer ingests dirt to connect him or herself with the desert (related to the subject matter) as well as to cause discomfort in both performer and audience (with the hopes of manifesting some catharsis). The mixture of dirt and saliva whilst speaking produces a mud byproduct the performer spits to both manifest said desert in the space and create evidence the performance occured.
